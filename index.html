Project introduce

population maps provide global information, evidence-based analytics, and knowledge on human presence on the planet. The ultimate goal of these maps is to ensure that every person is mapped and counted within critical operations such as policy making and disasters response. Due to the importance associated with world population maps, the assessment of accuracy and reliability of these datasets is key to inform any users on benefit and limitations deriving from their usage.

Countries

Global Human Settlement - Population Grid (GHS-POP)

This spatial raster dataset depicts the distribution and density of population,expressed as the number of people per cell.Global coverage datasets are available to download in Geotiff format with differentresolutions and CRS for years 1975, 1900, 2000, and 2015.
Target dataset: GHS-POP, epoch: 2015, CRS: WGS84, resolution: 30 arcsec.
WorldPop - Population Counts

Spatial raster dataset with the estimated total number of people per grid-cell. The global coverage dataset is available to download in Geotiff format at a resolution of 30 arcsec (CRS: WGS84) from the Year 2000 to 2020.
Target dataset: Population Counts/ Unconstrained global mosaics 2000/2020 (1 km resolution), year: 2015.
